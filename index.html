We, humans, are surprisingly good at learning from past experiences, even when compared to today’s most advanced artificial intelligence (AI) systems. Ironically, what makes us so special is also something that we are least aware of. This very mystery excites me and has driven my research in AI. In particular, my research interest lies in probablistic machine learning, with a focus on reinforcement learning, meta-learning, and lifelong learning.

Currently, I am a visiting research scholar hosted by Prof. Asif Ekbal at the AI-NLP-ML lab, IIT Patna. Here, I am currently focusing on the investigating lifelong learning and incremental learning (learning from a non-stationary stream of input data) with an emphasis on recurrent neural networks. During this time, I am also remotely collaborating on a joint project between Caltech and Facebook AI Research (FAIR), under the supervision of Dr. Kamyar Azizzadenesheli and Dr. Mohammad Ghavamzadeh. Here, my role is to design approximate-Bayesian variants of Policy Gradient and Actor-Critic algorithms that are more sample-efficient than their Monte-Carlo counterparts while maintaining a feasible computational complexity.
